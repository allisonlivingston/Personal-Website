Reflection

Currently, I have been learning Javascript. I had some knowledge of the topic having taken the Javascript Accelerated course, but the drills were still difficult. I was able to figure out the objectives of the drills and small pieces of the code fairly easily, but struggled mostly with determining how to put together those pieces of code to get the correct result. I drew input/output tables and wrote down, in words, what I was trying to accomplish with each drill. To move past my pain points with the drills, I first tried code that I could recall off the top of my head. If that didn't work, then I googled how to write code for parts of the function. Finally, I asked students and/or instructors for guidance when I was really stuck.

Though the drills took a long time, I learned new, interesting JS methods along the way that could be useful in the future and I enjoy the struggle! I look forward to learning more applications of JS functions and diving further into for and while loops. I generally like to figure things out on my own and avoid asking for help. I need to work on setting a time limit for struggling with a problem and recognizing when I'm stuck (aka entering the zone of proximal development) so I can ask for guidance sooner. Setting the stage at the beginning of my work period, however, allowed me to keep track of what code I had executed and stay headed in the right direction, rather than getting distracted and forgetting where I left off.
